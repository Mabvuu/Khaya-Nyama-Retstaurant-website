Our Story

The Secret to Our Success

The Beginning

The predeccesor of the current venue was created in 1984, when husband-and-wife team Duncan and Yvette Barker started off in what is now Sam Levy's Villag, moving in 1988 to the Ballantyne Park Shopping centre. In the 2000s , they decided to move to Australia and 2 years later returned to africa. They settled in Gauteng and opened a restaurant also formerly known as Wombles.

The couple decided to coe back home in 2020 and met up with Andrew Mama, owner of a successful upmarket restaurant called Amanzi, situated in the area where the Chisipite and Highlands surburbs meet. The Barkers being experienced restaurateurs, took over and rebranded a new venue called Wombles Khaya Nyama - the latter words meaning 'House of meat' in shona.

The Present

After opening Wombles, the youngest of their four daughters, Tiffany has been well trained in the trade by her father, Ducan. She keeps a special eye micro-managing the kitchen activity. Duncan unfortuately passed in 2021, leaving behind a distraught family but one that railed and carries on as a mother-and-daughter team of Yvette and Tiffany.

Yvette is in charge of front house and is getting to know an expanding clientele , while Tiffany focuses on kitchen and personally inspects everything heading out to tables. Ducan would be proud!
